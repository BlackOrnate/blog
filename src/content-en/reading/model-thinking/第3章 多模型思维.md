## 1. Condorcet's Jury Theorem and Diversity Prediction Theorem

- **Condorcet jury theorem** (#5__164)

  - Derived from a model that explains the strengths of the majority rule
  - By constructing multiple models and using the majority rule, it will be more accurate than using only one of the models
  - Relies on the concept of \*\*state of the world\*\* which is a complete description of all relevant information
  - If many models lead to similar results, then we have a powerful theorem that is largely independent of the details of the models
  - **Our truth is the intersection of a number of independent lies***
  - When many models give the same categorization, we gain confidence

## 5. Multi-model thinking

- Successful one-to-many thinking depends on creatively adapting assumptions and constructing new analogies in order to apply a model developed for a specific purpose to a new domain
- Becoming a multi-model thinker requires more than math skills, it requires creativity
